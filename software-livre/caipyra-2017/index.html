## Part 3: ## Licenses and Copyleft
![](images/copyleft.png) (from [Google Search](https://www.google.com.br/search?q=copyleft))
“Stallman worked a few years earlier on a Lisp interpreter. Symbolics asked to use the Lisp interpreter, and Stallman agreed to supply them with a public domain version of his work. Symbolics extended and improved the Lisp interpreter, but when Stallman wanted access to the improvements that Symbolics had made to his interpreter, Symbolics refused.”
-- Wikipedia
“GNU is not in the public domain. Everyone will be permitted to modify and redistribute GNU, but no distributor will be allowed to restrict its further redistribution. That is to say, proprietary modifications will not be allowed. I want to make sure that all versions of GNU remain free.”
-- Richard Stallman on GNU Manifesto, 1985
## General Public License ![](images/gplv3.png) [gnu.org/licenses/gpl-3.0.en.html](https://www.gnu.org/licenses/gpl-3.0.en.html)
## Copyleft - License feature - Forces the software to be **always free/libre** - **Guarantees user freedom**
## Problems of non-copylefted software - Symbolics and the Lisp interpreter - Microsoft Visual Studio Code (spyware) - Apple (darwin) - *Put your cool non-copylefted open-source software here*
## If you don't know the differences you may be helping your enimies
## Licensing tips - As a developer: read the major FLOSS licenses - If you'd like to support the ecosystem, go with copyleft - As a user: read carefully the terms (eg: Microsoft Visual Studio Code)
